Models for Classical Computation

To start off, when computer scientists say computing, they really meant some form of machines (computer) that can solve problem. Some of which are:

  • Turing Machine
  • Finite Automata
  • $\lambda$-calulus
  • Coin
  • Soap Bubbles and Pegs (my favourite, read the paper by Scott Aaronson for more info)
  •  $\vdots$

 In 1936 Turing came up with the famous Turing Universal Computation. At that time, Turing was interested in solving some obscure problems related with the decision problems and he noticed that some model of computation are universal, in other word, they can sufficiently simulate other models of computation.
